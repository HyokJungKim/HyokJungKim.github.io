Job Market Paper

  • Consumption and Savings under Imperfect Perception of Wealth

    Dispersion of liquid wealth, which has homogeneous return, is still severe when controlled for education or income classes. To capture this dispersion, I build a model with a heterogeneous perception of expenditure shocks. The model predicts a positive relationship between the degree of pessimism and the accumulation of wealth. I show that even small expenditure shocks, on average, can generate a large dispersion of wealth. When calibrating the distribution of perception to match the level of liquid wealth at different percentiles, the model generates a monthly MPC of 23 percent and the Gini coefficient on the liquid wealth of 0.62 even with the IES of 1/3. The result is robust to different choices of IES.
