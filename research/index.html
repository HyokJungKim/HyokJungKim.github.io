Job Market Paper

  • Consumption and Savings under Imperfect Perception of Expenditure Shocks

    In this paper, I build a life cycle model of consumption and savings where households face exogenous expenditure shocks. Households are heterogeneous as they have different levels of perceptions of expenditure shocks. Households who underestimate the expenditure shocks tend to spend more now and save less for the future. The calibration result matches the dispersion of liquid wealth in data, with many households underestimating the future expenditure shocks. The model exhibits high marginal propensity to consume as reported in the empirical evidence even with the intertemporal elasticity of substitution far below one. This high marginal propensity to consume is due to a large number of hand-to-mouth households resulting from their underestimation of future expenditure shocks. The model also exhibits a a realistic level of liquid wealth over the life cycle. I also provide a policy recommendation that can enhance overall welfare.
